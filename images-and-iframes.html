About Cristiano Ronaldo

Cristiano Ronaldo, born in Madeira, Portugal in 1985,
rose from humble beginnings to become one of the greatest footballers of
all time. Known for his speed, skill, and relentless drive, he began his
professional career with Sporting CP before making a name at
Manchester United. His legendary stint at Real Madrid saw him break
countless records and win multiple Champions League titles.
Later, he played for Juventus, returned to Manchester United, and
currently stars for Al-Nassr. A five-time Ballon d’Or winner and Portugal’s
all-time top scorer, Ronaldo’s legacy is defined by his unmatched work ethic,
influence, and love for the beautiful game.



Ronaldo's Celebrations

The “Siuuu” celebration started around 2013 when Ronaldo played for Real Madrid. During a game in the U.S.,

he shouted “Sii!” (Spanish for “Yes!”) after scoring, and fans loved it. He kept doing it,

adding the spin and jump later. Since then, it’s become his signature move and a symbol of his success.


Ronaldo's Ionic move

The Ronaldo Chop is one of Cristiano Ronaldo’s signature moves. He uses it to quickly change direction and

trick defenders. To do it, he jumps slightly, swings one leg across his body, and cuts the ball behind his other

leg. It’s fast, flashy, and great for beating opponents, messi is better especially when running down the wing.



Club's Ronaldo has Played with
Sporting CP (2002-2003)

Cristiano Ronaldo began his professional career with Sporting CP in Portugal. His impressive performances there caught the attention of top European clubs, leading to his transfer to Manchester United in 2003.


Manchester United (2003-2009)

In 2003, Cristiano Ronaldo made a high-profile move to Manchester United. Under the guidance of Sir Alex Ferguson, he developed into a world-class player, winning three Premier League titles and his first Ballon d’Or in 2008.


Real Madrid (2002-2003)

Cristiano Ronaldo transferred to Real Madrid in 2009 for a then-world record fee. During his time at Madrid, he became the club’s all-time leading scorer, won four Champions League titles, and secured four more Ballon d’Or awards.


Sporting CP (2018-2022)

>In 2018, Cristiano Ronaldo joined Juventus in Italy. He continued to showcase his goal-scoring prowess, helping Juventus win two Serie A titles and further cementing his legacy as one of football’s all-time greats.


Manchester United (2022-2023)

In 2022, Cristiano Ronaldo made a sensational return to Manchester United. Although his second stint was brief, he continued to demonstrate his goal-scoring ability and leadership on the pitch.


Al Nassr (2023-2027)

In late 2022, Cristiano Ronaldo joined Al-Nassr in Saudi Arabia. This move marked a new chapter in his illustrious career, where he continues to play and contribute to the growth of football in the region.
